How quickly will I be able to play songs?:
Depends. Generally longer for people who are older, but still...depends.

How often do I need to practice?:
It is advisable to practice 15 minutes a day, 5 times a week. This is the optimum time as it ensures maximum practice while simultaneously ensuring that you don’t burn out.

My fingers hurt, my hands are not quick enough, should i take a break?:
Yes, take a break. Let the muscles or the skin cells thicken. But do not give up.

What type of guitars exist in this universe we call our reality. Which one should I get?:
The basic types are
i) Electric (including hollow bodied or semi-acoustics)
ii) Acoustic (steel strings - including those with electrics fitted, so called electro acoustics)
iii) Classical (nylon strings).

I am of the opinion for a beginner an electric guitar is the best option, mainly because they are a little easier to play and hence encouraging in terms of results.
Here is a stream of consciousness:
• Wanna play rock: buy an electric.
• Wanna play classical: buy a nylon string classical guitar.
• Wanna play folk: buy a steel string acoustic.
• Aren’t really sure, get an electric.

Here are some tips that will help you make the best possible use of the time you so graciously give for practice:
1. Practice what you can't do, not what you can.
2. Never practice a mistake. Strive to get it right.
3. Start slow and perfect it before you speed up.
4. Using a timer actually saves time.
5. Pay attention to one element at a time.
6. Practice a little every day, rather than giving your everything on a single day
7. Track your practice: use a schedule.
8. Don’t be harsh on yourself, if it sounds good, it is good!
9. Playing and Practicing are very different, keep that in mind.
10. Strive to become instinctive.
